Project Ideas

A beats per minute (BMP) music matching prototype APP that matches an input in which will be avg BPM with a music tempo or BMP. The input will collect and be categorized into a number set range of BPM for example 50-60, 60-70, 70-80, going up to 200 in which it will then correspond to a song that falls into that range of BPM. The goal then would be to further develop the prototype in which one could collect input with a device such as a heart rate (HR) watch or chest strap monitor and match it with music that falls into that range.

This app would be great for people who are into exercising or need something to give them more motivation when they are doing their training. “Studies have shown that listening to high-tempo music increases the benefits of exercise for physical fitness and reduces the perceived effort involved” (Hastings 2020, para. 2). Currently Spotify has a tool called Simply Spotify Running. This tool uses your phone sensors to detect your steps per minute and match music to that running tempo. Users find the app advantageous but feel like it there is room for improvement (Mitroff 2015). I think tracking something like BPM would be helpful in improving this function.

The app will focus on the development of the protype first in which will be done using a programming platform or an app developing platform. The logical part of the app will be made up of a library, input collection, data analyzing and output. The library will store songs into their respected BPM categories. The songs will be categorized into sets of 10, for example 50-60, 60-70, 70-80BMP etc. going up to 200BPM. There are already BMP song analysis tools that could be used to collect the BPM of the songs, these include tools such as songbpm (SongBPM 2021). and GetSongbpm (GetSongbpm 2020).The input for the prototype will be from a keyboard, by tapping your space bar to get avg BMP. This will then be paired with a matching song in that range. After the prototype is developed the goal would be to look at pairing it with HR monitors such as a HR monitoring watch or chest monitoring strap that has its own BPM analyzer. This would require more research, and working with other companies possibly, to make the platforms compatible. The Data analyzing component will first store the current BPM and set it in its respective range. The range is set in sets of 10 to give the music a bit of a buffer and range to work in, otherwise the music would undergo change too quickly and make it too hard for the user to hold a BPM intensity of +-1s. This may require tweaking along the way and the buffer may need to be tweaked. The output will play the matching song on an audio output device such as headphones or speakers and will undergo change when the intensity of the BMP changes.

I think the goal would support both Android and IOS mobile devices. This would require looking at low-level coding. To develop code for the prototype, using low-level code to support IOS I could use Swift and for Android I could use Java. This would allow the prototype to be more customizable, but would require a higher level of skill for to development of the app. I could however create a hybrid code using leagues such as Cordova or React Native which would be compatible with both Andriod and IOS. This however might affect the customization of the app (buildfire n.d.).

If the prototype is successful, the goal would be further development and making it compatible with current technologies such a HR monitoring devices and music apps such as Spotify or apple music. The prototype could also be sold to anyone of the companies as well. This app would further increase the accuracy of matching a songs BPMs with ones current input of BPMs. The effect of this could help motivate and push someone harder in their fitness training.
